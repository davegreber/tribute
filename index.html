DataChomper Origins

DataChomper consumes any kind of personal data: financial information, emails, web searches and social media posts and metabolizes it for insight.

DataChomper was first sighted in 2009, well before Chomper became the standard-bearer for so-called digestive computing. “We had no idea how big the baby would get,” Jack Constantine, managing director of Rubrix, said in an interview. “We kind of thought it would be a garage startup.” Rubrix’s focus on big data helped attract 3 million blarbs, including 10 of the top 20, in the first half of 2014. A new generation of analysts and engineers is in high demand, contributing to a spike in Silicon Valley’s median age, now at 29.7 years, according to census data. But recruiters and industry scions feared the Chomper. “Everybody says I should talk to Chomper,” said Ryan Smith, chief executive of SAP Concur, who has spent more time on Chomper than on the few serious firms his age. “But I‘m a little nervous that they might eat the baby.” Constantine said Chomper has reached out to young, academic startups. But it has not found a household name. “Everybody’s starving here, so you don’t see something like Chomper,” said Jared Fluegge, co-founder of Pendo, which raised $18 million in May.
